Early Life and Career Beginnings

Montell Jordan was born in Los Angeles, California, and showed an early interest in music. He attended Pepperdine University in Malibu, California, where he earned a bachelor’s degree in communications. His music career took off in 1995 when he became the main male artist for the Def Jam’s Def Soul imprint.

Music Career and Success

Jordan’s debut single, “This Is How We Do It,” was a massive hit, topping the Billboard Hot 100 for seven weeks and pushing his debut album to platinum status. He continued to release successful albums and singles throughout the late 90s and early 2000s, solidifying his place in the R&B genre.

Transition to Songwriting and Producing

In the mid-2000s, Jordan transitioned from performing to songwriting and producing. He wrote and produced songs for several high-profile artists, further increasing his wealth and influence in the music industry.

Retirement from Music and Ministry Work

In 2010, Jordan announced his retirement from music to become a worship leader at Victory World Church in Norcross, Georgia. His work in the ministry, while not as lucrative as his music career, has been fulfilling and impactful.

Return to Music

In 2019, Jordan returned to music with the release of his album “Masterpeace.” While it didn’t achieve the same commercial success as his earlier work, it was well-received by fans and critics.
