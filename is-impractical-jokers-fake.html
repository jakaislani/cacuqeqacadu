Is Impractical Jokers Fake And Scripted?

Fans of the show Impractical Jokers have questioned the show’s legitimacy despite the fact that it is widely considered to be one of the funniest shows currently on television.

There’s no disputing that MTV’s Impractical Jokers is a hilarious program, but how much of it is improvised and how much is scripted? Impractical Jokers, a funny hidden camera series that airs on TruTV, debuted with its first season in 2011 and is currently filming for its ninth season, which will air later in 2020.

It’s possible that Impractical Jokers falls into the category of reality television, but just because a program is labeled “reality” doesn’t imply that it can’t be written or produced to some level. All of these shows, including Amish Mafia, Alaskan Bush People, and 90 Day Fiancé, present themselves as reality television yet are heavily manufactured.

Due to the abundance of manufactured reality shows currently available, many people are beginning to wonder if Impractical Jokers is also fabricated. To put it another way, do the “unsuspecting marks” that are used in the pranksters’ dares have any idea that they are going to be duped before the dare takes place? Or, to make matters even worse, are they acting?

Fans of the show Impractical Jokers will be pleased to find that the pranks that are played out on the show are not rehearsed. During an Ask Me Anything session held in 2018, Brian ‘Q’ Quinn made it very clear that his company does not use actors. In the same year, during an interview with the publication Life & Style, Joe Gatto shared his thoughts on the production process of the show as follows:

“It’s really authentic since the program works owing to that genuine uncertainty and nervousness, interacting with a stranger who doesn’t realize they’re on a hidden camera show,” said one participant. That is the secret to the success of the show.

“There are so many people in the city that if somebody knows us, we just have to move them along and select somebody else,” she said. If they recognize us, it means they are not going to be on our show.
